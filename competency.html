Core Competencies

The core competencies of an organization are the specific things that it does best. It is important to understand the core competencies of an organization in order to capitalize and leverage on those aspects in its marketing plan in order to multiply its outreach potential. After a weeklong dialogue and engagement with the WEF team, the Foundation’s core competencies have been identified to be the following:

Global Network of Education Researchers

The Foundation has access to world- class educators who are pursuing research in various education related disciplines. The founder himself has been an impactful educator with a leadership role for over a decade in leading English medium schools in Bangladesh like Scholastica and Aga Khan School. He is currently pursuing his post graduate studies researching on multimodal learning practices specializing on education for the marginalized population under the supervision of Dr. Jennifer Rowsell, Canada research chair for multiliteracies.

Global Network of Support Systems

The Foundation has also successfully built a global support system by which it is connected to social activists, volunteers, sponsors and media & marketing support from around the world. For example: The Foundation has already employed a North American Project Director, Macy Tuollefson at the Gazipur Branch, who has extensive work experience in the field of development.

Differentiating Factor

Every organization has a competitive advantage that helps it survive against competition. A competitive advantage can either be Cost or Differentiation. If a company for example can produce its goods and services at a competitive cost, its competitive advantage is then Cost, which means the company will be able to charge a lower price than its competition. As a result consumers would be more likely to buy their products over their competitors’. Or if the company can produce goods and services that are unique and differentiated in a way that cannot be copied by others (for example through a patent), then it has a competitive advantage in differentiation, because consumers will only find such product through that company.

WEF as a non-profit has a competitive advantage that revolves around differentiation. The aspect that differentiates it from the rest of the non-profits working in the education sector is its 3-leveled Literacy Model. This model incorporates a concept called Multimodal Learning Practices at the highest level of the pyramid. In a culture of memorization-based literacy, such a model not only stands-out but provides a more holistic and competent form of education. The primary objective of this model is to build thought leadership among students.

The Foundation has successfully managed to draw attention to North American Educational and development work researchers. It currently recruits interns from North American unvierisities providing world class work.
